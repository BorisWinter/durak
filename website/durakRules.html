Durak Rules

Introduction to Durak's rules

We play Durak in this simulation with very simplified rules. You can find a "playthrough" demonstration of the simple rules of the game below. We play with 3 suits, and cards are ranked according to number and trump suit. If Clubs is the trump suit, the ranking is as follows: C4 > C3 > C2 > S4 = H4 > S3 = H3 > S2 = H2.

Playing Durak

Start of the Game All players have their cards dealt to them. In our basic game, the handlimit is two, meaning that every player has 2 cards. Players can only see their own cards. One of the cards in the deck is visible to the players (in this illustration, that's the 3 of Clubs), and its suit indicates the trump suit. The other cards in the deck are unknown to the players. The discard pile is empty.
First attack The first player is selected randomly, in this case, it's North. This player does not have any information about the player they are attacking (South), so they follow the appropriate strategy.
Successful Defence Player South can successfully defend against the attack by North by playing a card with a higher value. A player has to defend if they can.
After a successful defence Both cards go to the discard pile and "disappear" from the game.
Hand limit As both player's hand size is lower than the hand limit of 2, they both draw a new card from the deck.
Next attack Player South now attacks player West (not according to the strategy, as West plays the highest value in the game. This behaviour would not happen in our simulated games, but would happen in real-life games. In this case this was done to illustrate an unsuccessful defence in the next step.).
Player West tries to defend Player West sees that they cannot win against Player South's card.
Unsuccessful defence Player West is unsuccessful and takes both cards in the attack field in their hand, as they could not defend.
Player South Hand Limit Player South has too few cards in hand now, and draws the final card from the deck. After this, players cannot draw any card from the deck anymore, and cards can only be removed to the discard pile. The player that reaches zero cards in hand first, wins!
